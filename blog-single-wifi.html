How WiFi Works?

When you are outside of home like in an airport, cafe, park, library or hotel recently, then you might have experienced the wireless technology. The wireless networking, also called WiFi or 802.11 networking is basically used to connect their computers, mobiles, laptops, or other wireless accessories. Wireless networking In the near future, wireless networking may become so widespread that you can access the Internet just about anywhere at any time, without the use of cables.

WiFi, reliable wireless technology

Wi-Fi is the name of a wireless networking technology that generally uses radio waves to provide wireless high-speed Internet and network connections. There is a common misconception that the term Wi-Fi is short for "wireless fidelity," however it is not true. Wi-Fi is simply a trademarked phrase that means IEEE 802.11x. There is no physicial connection between sender and receiver. The connction is established by using radio frequency (RF) technology -- a frequency within the electromagnetic spectrum associated with radio wave propagation. When an RF current is supplied to an antenna, an electromagnetic field is created that then is able to propagate through space. The cornerstone of any wireless network is an access point (AP). The main job of an access point is to broadcast a wireless signal that the devices equipped with wireless adapters can detect and "tune" into. WiFi frequency bands are frequency ranges within the wireless spectrum that are designated to carry WiFi. There are two types of WiFi frequency bands, they are: 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz.
